# Downloaded Papers

## Core Papers (Most Relevant)

1. **On the Utility of Learning about Humans for Human-AI Coordination** (carroll2019_overcooked_ai.pdf)
   - Authors: Carroll, Shah, Ho, Griffiths, Seshia, Abbeel, Dragan
   - Year: 2019 (NeurIPS)
   - arXiv: 1910.05789
   - Why relevant: Introduces Overcooked-AI benchmark for cooperative coordination; shows conventions develop during self-play

2. **Emergent Coordination in Multi-Agent Language Models** (riedl2025_emergent_coordination_llm.pdf)
   - Authors: Riedl
   - Year: 2026 (ICLR)
   - arXiv: 2510.05174
   - Why relevant: Information-theoretic framework for measuring emergent coordination; shows persona+ToM prompting creates Schelling point convergence

3. **Other-Play for Zero-Shot Coordination** (hu2020_other_play_zsc.pdf)
   - Authors: Hu, Lerer, Peysakhovich, Foerster

   - arXiv: 2003.02979
   - Why relevant: Breaking arbitrary conventions during training produces transferable coordination

4. **The Hanabi Challenge: A New Frontier for AI Research** (bard2019_hanabi_challenge.pdf)
   - Authors: Bard, Foerster, Chandar, et al.
   - Year: 2020
   - arXiv: 1902.00506
   - Why relevant: Hanabi as a game that naturally teaches convention formation and Theory of Mind

   - Authors: Lauffer, Shah, Carroll, Dennis, Russell
